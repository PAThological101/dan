Hey Viv. The answer is no.

p=NS for secondary endpoints in an NI study never means non-inferiority.

Two key things to keep in mind:

  1. The appropriate NI margin is dependent on the endpoint & population (these are rarely stated a-priori for secondary endpoints).
  2. An inference of non-inferiority is actually a statistically significant finding (not p=NS)
