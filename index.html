Issue Group 3.5 - ENDPOINT CONSIDERATIONS: Secondary endpoints.

It is the results for the PRIMARY endpoint that are intended to address the main study objective.

SEE MORE...

The target sample size is therefore calculated based on the number of primary endpoint events that would be required to reject the null hypothesis and the expected occurrence rate of those events (i.e., the study is powered for the primary endpoint). Many other aspects of study planning are determined (or at least impacted) by the primary endpoint (e.g., the study duration, inclusion and exclusion criteria, measurement protocols, and so on).

However, trials are very expensive to conduct (particularly RCTs), so there is financial incentive to include more than one endpoint per trial. Additionally, because most diseases and conditions have more than one clinical consequence, efficacy cannot be adequately established on the basis of a single endpoint. Hence the need for secondary endpoints. Although they are born out of need, they come with important limitations that must be considered when interpreting their results. For example:

  1. Secondary endpoints are usually not taken into consideration from a power standpoint when designing a trial
  2. The study cannot possibly be tailored in a manner promoting their validity to the same extent that the study is tailored to the primary endpoint
  3. There are generally MANY more secondary endpoints than primary endpoints

As a result of issues 1-3, secondary endpoints must be viewed with caution. This is why it is so important to prominently disclose which study results relate to secondary endpoints in HCP advertising. Note that issue 3 justifies heightened skepticism about statistically significant findings in secondary endpoints. Additionally, issues 1 and 2 explain why a non-significant finding on the primary endpoint would be interpreted as a "failed study" while a non-significant finding in a secondary endpoint would be interpreted as inconclusive. This will be discussed further throughout this group of review issues.

3.5.3 - APS presentation does not adequately differentiate between the primary and secondary endpoints.

Understanding

The HCP should be able to identify which endpoints are not primary endpoints.
more...

SEE MORE...

The advertiser can choose from one of the following three ways to differentiate secondary endpoints from primary endpoints:

  1. Identify the primary endpoint in the APS data presentation by labeling it “Primary endpoint.” This conveys that the other endpoints in that presentation are not primary.
  2. Identify the secondary endpoints in the APS data presentation by labeling them (or presenting them in a context that conveys) “secondary endpoint.”
  3. Precede the APS data presentation with a protocol description listing out the various “Primary” and “Secondary” endpoints for study.
